About

Dr Encarni Medina-Lopez is a Senior Lecturer in Ocean Observation at the University of Edinburgh. She is Principal Investigator of the FeME Network Plus project, Deputy Director for Training at SENSE, and Co-investigator at IDCORE. She is affiliated with the Alan Turing Institute and the University of Granada.

Research

Her research focuses on remote sensing of coastal environments, marine renewable energy, satellite data applications, and machine learning for environmental monitoring. She leads and collaborates on projects such as FeME Network Plus, CEReS Group, SENSE, and IDCORE.

Publications

  • Satellite data for the offshore renewable energy sector: Synergies and innovation opportunities. Remote Sensing of Environment, 2021.
  • The use of satellite products to assess spatial uncertainty and reduce life-time costs of offshore wind farms. Cleaner Environmental Systems, 2021.
  • Machine Learning for Detection of Macroalgal Blooms in the Mar Menor Coastal Lagoon Using Sentinel-2 Remote Sensing. Remote Sensing, 2023.

Teaching

Dr Medina-Lopez teaches courses in ocean observation and remote sensing, and supervises PhD and MSc students. She is actively involved in outreach and mentoring activities.

Group

The Coastal and Environmental Remote Sensing (CEReS) Group focuses on satellite-based monitoring of coastal and marine environments. The group collaborates on interdisciplinary projects and offers opportunities for research and training.
