About

Dr Encarni Medina-Lopez is a Senior Lecturer in Ocean Observation at the University of Edinburgh. Her research focuses on remote sensing to study water, energy, and environmental problems, using satellite imagery, in situ data, and machine learning. She holds a PhD in Energy Systems & Dynamics of Biogeochemical Flows and is a Turing Fellow, Chartered Engineer, and certified drone pilot.

Research

Her research includes satellite data applications for marine renewable energy, coastal morphology, and climate change. She leads the CEReS group and is involved in projects like SCORE, FeME Network Plus, and IDCORE. She supervises multiple PhD and MSc students on topics ranging from glacier collapse to offshore solar resource assessment.

Publications

Selected publications include work on multispectral satellite imagery, machine learning for shoreline indicators, and LCOE modeling for wave energy. A full list is available on her Google Scholar profile.

Teaching

Dr Medina-Lopez teaches undergraduate and postgraduate courses including Programming Skills for Engineers, Potable Water Supply, and workshops on Python and Google Earth Engine for the SENSE CDT.

Group

The Coastal and Environmental Remote Sensing Group (CEReS) studies remote sensing of the coastal ocean, marine renewables, and coastal engineering. The group includes postdoctoral fellows and PhD students working on diverse environmental and engineering challenges.
